Facts about Antipolo City

Tipolo Trees

Antipolo City was founded by Miguel de Legazpi in 1570. The Spanish governor-general named the place after the abundance of "Tipolo" trees in the area. In 1591, the construction started for the original church that will later on become the Antipolo Cathedral.

Arch of Antipolo

It served as the capital of Morong for a short while. During the First Republic, Antipolo was considered as the capital city of Morong. The Revolutionary Government during then moved its capital to Tanay. In 1901, Antipolo became one of the first cities in the newly established Rizal Province.

Old photo of Antipolo City

Antipolo became a place of refuge during WW2. When the Japanese invaded the country, many Filipinos sought refuge on Antipolo city. When it was invaded, it took over 12 days of blood and bombings before Antipolo became liberated from the Japanese.

Map of Modern Rizal Province

Post-war Transformation. In 1947, after the Philippines declared its independence, the Antipolo Cathedral was rebuilt. In 1998, Antipolo became a city, and was declared a highly-urbanized city in 2011. In 2020, it officially became the capital of Rizal, replacing Pasig.

Antipolo is home to over 924,000 individuals.

Also known as the "Pilgrimage City", it is home to the revered image of the Virgin Mary housed in the Antipolo Cathedral since 1626.

Pilgrimage

Thousands of devotees visit the National Shrine of Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage, also known as the Antipolo Cathedral, to attend mass and offer prayers to the Blessed Mother throughout the month of May, which is also dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary. The Maytime Pilgrimage Season in Antipolo City will begin with the customary "Walk For Peace" of followers and pilgrims of Nuestra Señora de la Paz Y Buenviaje, also known as the Virgin of Antipolo, or Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage. Similar to the events that take place every Holy Thursday, pilgrims and devoteees will join the Walk for Peace to the Antipolo Cathedral, with the pilgrim image encased in its glass-covered "carroza" like vehicle leading the thousands of walking devotees.

SuMaKaH Festival

The SuMaKah Festival, which is an acronym for the four main products of Antipolo - Suman, Mangga, Kasoy, and Hamaka - begins on May 1st. It is part of the Maytime Festival, a month-long celebration honoring Antipolo's patroness, Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage. This yearly event seeks to build unity between the local government and the community while showcasing the rich culture and traditions of the Antipoleños to the entire world. These colorful festivities include the Unang Prusisyon, Santacruzan and Katapusang Prusisyon, together with the beautiful street dancing competitions.

Pinto Art Museum

Established in 2010 by Dr. Joven Cuanang, Pinto Art Museum is a beautiful place for art lovers. It's set in a calm 2-hectare garden and has a variety of artworks like sculptures, installations, mixed media, and paintings. The museum displays the skills of Filipino artists, exploring different modern styles, religious stories, and historical events. The museum's lovely Santorini-like design makes it an ideal spot for taking amazing photos for your social media.

Located in: 1 Sierra Madre St, Grand Heights Subdivision, Antipolo, 1870 Rizal

Hinulugang Taktak

Hinulugang Taktak Protected Landscape, commonly known as Hinulugang Taktak, is a preserved area situated in Antipolo. It features the popular Hinulugang Taktak false. Originally intended as a recreational space, the waterfall locale earned its status as a national park through Republic Act No. 6964 in 1990 named "An Act Converting the Hinulugan Taktak Recreation Area in the Municipality of Antipolo, Province of Rizal, into a National Park to be known as the Hinulugan Taktak National Park and Appropriating Funds therefor".

Cloud 9

A popular recreational spot is the Cloud 9 Sports and Leisure Club, initially launched as a restaurant in 1995. Over time, it expanded to offer accommodations and various recreational and event facilities. It's main highlight is the 360-degree viewing deck that offers a full panoramic view of Metro Manila from the Antipolo mountains.

Located in: 23 Sumulong Highway, Padilla, Antipolo, 1870 Rizal

Antipolo Cathedral

The Antipolo Cathedral church was finished in 1632, but it faced a serious damage in 1639 when it was victim to a fire caused by the Sangleys (Chinese). It got fixed but soon damaged due to the 1645 Luzon earthquake. It experienced more earthquakes in 1824 and 1863 that caused significant damage to the church.

Located in: Dela Paz Street, Antipolo, 1870 Rizal

PACEM Eco Park

Situated within the premises of Assumption Antipolo, it was created to foster environmental awareness among local and foreign visitors. It offers a delightful blend of attractions, including a mini-forest, a wildlife sanctuary, an aviary, and an organic garden. Additionally, the park houses a captivating museum that carefully exhibits butterflies originating from various parts of the Philippines and Asia.

Located in: Sumulong Highway, Antipolo, 1870 Rizal
Open in Google Maps

Via Dolo Rosa

Formerly known as "The White Cross", the location has been rebranded as Via Dolo Rosa at Pinagmisan St. in Antipolo City. This hill with grotto-like features has transformed into a breathtaking pilgrimage spot where regular masses and "alay-lakads" (offering walks) are conducted. At the peak of Pinagmisahan Hills, one an find the impressive White Cros and a large statue of Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage. As pilgrims make their way up the hill, they encounter life-sized depictions of the stations of the cross, symbolizing the sacred journey of Jesus Christ, encompassing his passion, death, and resurrectio. Renamed Via Dolo Rosa, the site holds special significance as a place for spiritual contemplation and devotion.

Located in: H5R8+845, Pinagmisahan Street, Antipolo, 1870 Rizal
